## Overview

The team discussed improving the main company website, with a focus on brand direction, SEO, messaging clarity, and visual design. There was broad agreement that the current site needs significant improvement before deeper implementation work continues.

## Key Points

- The current website design and typography need work; even a simple font update would be an improvement.
- SEO is a concern, especially around whether the site content is readable and indexable properly.
- The website copy was confirmed to be handwritten, not AI-generated.
- Server-side rendering was discussed as a likely requirement for better SEO performance.
- Before making major technical or visual updates, the team wants to define the desired brand look and feel.
- Textured backgrounds were discussed as a possible visual direction, with Tavis referenced as an example.
- The team felt some reference sites have too much going on and make it unclear what the product actually does.
- Clearer messaging is a priority: visitors should quickly understand what the company offers without needing to read dense paragraphs.
- Lemon Slice was mentioned as a stronger example of clearly communicating the product and highlighting key differentiators.

## Decisions / Direction

- Establish the brand direction first, then move quickly on implementation.
- Prioritize clarity over visual complexity.
- Remove unnecessary sections or design elements that distract from the core message.
- Use reference sites to evaluate what works, especially around above-the-fold messaging and product clarity.

## Action Items

- Define the desired visual identity for the site.
- Decide whether textured backgrounds fit the brand.
- Review SEO setup and confirm whether server-side rendering is working correctly.
- Improve the website copy so the product value is immediately clear.
- Update typography and visual styling once the brand direction is agreed on.
- Audit the current homepage for unnecessary or confusing sections.
